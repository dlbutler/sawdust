fixed, fluid, and elastic

as far as i can make out, there seem to be three main philosophies of presentation.

fixed

fixed widths are good for ensuring that your design always looks the same. to create a fixed layout, you use pixels as your unit. pixels seem to be the easiest to understand and when translating from an existing design, it's much more straightforward. the problem with an absolute layout is that if any one of the many variables inherent in web design changes, there's no telling what will happen to your page. (or what won't happen.)

elastic

elastic layouts are designed to look good no matter the zoom of the viewport. from what i've read, it seems like this is no longer such an important capability. however, the emphasis on using ems as a unit seems to be the most lasting influence on designs today.
